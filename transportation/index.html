Island of TANITI

Transportation

Transportation: The majority of visitors arrive in Taniti by air, with a small number arriving aboard a cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay once a week. The island is serviced by a small airport, equipped to handle small jets and propeller planes. Plans are currently underway to expand the airport, allowing for larger jets to land on Taniti within the next few years.

Ground Transportation: For local travel, public buses operate in Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily, while private buses cover other parts of the island. Taxis are readily available within Taniti City. For those preferring more independence, rental cars can be obtained from an agency near the airport. Additionally, bikes complete with helmets (mandatory by law) can be rented from several vendors. Taniti City's flat terrain makes it highly walkable, and many tourists find the area around Merriton Landing particularly pedestrian-friendly, offering a delightful exploration experience on foot.
